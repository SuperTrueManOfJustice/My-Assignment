Meanings of the word "Void"

(Empty Space)

Definition: A completely empty space or the state of being empty.
Example: "The void between the stars is filled with darkness."

(Invalid)

Definition: Something that is not legally binding or has no legal effect.
Example: "The contract was declared void due to a lack of signatures."

(Lacking)

Definition: The absence of something or a feeling of emptiness.
Example: "After his departure, she felt a void in her heart."

(Nullify)

Definition: To make something invalid or cancel it.
Example: "The judge decided to void the previous ruling."

Conclusion: Understanding the different nuances of "void" can help you use it more effectively in various contexts. Whether describing an empty space, a legal nullity, or an emotional gap, "void" is a versatile word that conveys a sense of absence or emptiness.
